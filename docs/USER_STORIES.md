# User Stories - Seattle Source Ranker

Sarah is a technical recruiter at a major Seattle tech company. Sarah wants to discover influential open-source projects maintained by local developers to identify potential hiring candidates. She needs quick filtering by programming language, clear project metrics, and direct access to developer profiles. Sarah has moderate technical knowledge and values a clean, intuitive interface with straightforward navigation.

Alex is a software engineer who actively contributes to open-source projects. Alex wants to find high-quality Seattle-area projects to contribute to and network with local developers. He needs detailed project health indicators, browsing by language preference, and the ability to discover promising projects beyond the top-ranked ones. Alex is a highly skilled developer with 5+ years of experience who values accurate scoring algorithms and data transparency.

Maria is a technology journalist covering the Seattle tech scene. Maria wants to identify trending open-source projects for articles and track the local tech ecosystem's growth. She needs comprehensive statistics, easy identification of top projects across languages, and quick access to developer information for interviews. Maria has basic technical literacy and needs a straightforward interface with clear visualizations.

Muwen is a graduate student in mathematics at the University of Washington. He wants to explore local open-source projects to learn real-world coding practices and understand developer influence. He needs access to clear project metrics, contribution trends, and language comparisons to support his analysis. Muwen is technically skilled but values simple, transparent tools that make data easy to interpret.

Kevin is a data engineer at a Seattle startup building internal developer tools. He identifies high-quality open-source projects to learn architecture patterns and discover potential libraries to integrate. For technology selection decisions he will review project maturity metrics and maintainer activity levels. For deeper evaluation, he needs access to contribution patterns and code quality indicators to assess long-term viability. Kevin is highly technical and knows how to evaluate software dependencies and assess community health.

Jessica is a technical recruiter at Amazon focusing on senior engineering roles. She evaluates candidates by analyzing their open-source contributions and community impact in the Seattle area. For initial screening she will filter by specific languages like Python or Go and review star counts. For final interviews, she needs detailed contribution history and cross-project influence metrics. Jessica has moderate technical knowledge and knows how to assess code quality and developer activity patterns.

Michael is a bootcamp student at Code Fellows learning full-stack development. He discovers local projects to contribute to as part of building his portfolio and networking with Seattle developers. For learning purposes he will search for beginner-friendly projects with active maintainers. For career development, he needs access to project activity indicators and contributor contact information. Michael has basic programming skills and values clear project descriptions and approachable contribution guidelines.

Lisa is a tech community organizer planning meetups and networking events in Seattle. She identifies active local developers and trending projects to invite as speakers and showcase at events. For regular meetups she will browse popular projects across different languages to find diverse speakers. For flagship events, she needs detailed metrics to identify rising stars and influential maintainers in the community. Lisa has moderate technical knowledge and knows how to evaluate project impact and developer engagement levels.

Emily is a computer science student at Seattle University researching developer ecosystems. She analyzes open-source trends and community structures in local tech hubs for her thesis project. For class assignments she will explore language-specific communities and basic metrics. For her thesis, she needs comprehensive historical data and the ability to track developer migration patterns across projects. Emily is technically proficient and knows how to process datasets and apply statistical analysis methods.

Brandon is a contract recruiter specializing in startup placements across Seattle. He identifies technical co-founders and early employees by discovering developers with strong open-source track records. For client searches he will filter by multiple criteria including language expertise and project leadership roles. For candidate pitches, he needs quick access to portfolio highlights and verifiable contribution metrics. Brandon has limited technical background but understands GitHub terminology and how to evaluate developer reputation.

David is a systems administrator at a mid-sized Seattle tech company managing a team of 50 developers. He evaluates team members' external contributions and skill development by tracking their open-source activities in the local community. For performance reviews he will examine contribution frequency and project diversity across team members. For skill gap analysis, he needs access to language distribution metrics and trending technologies in local projects. David is highly technical and knows how to assess developer productivity and technical growth patterns.

Rachel is a technical consultant who performs due diligence for companies acquiring Seattle startups. She evaluates the technical capabilities of acquisition targets by analyzing their engineering teams' open-source portfolios. For initial assessments she will review aggregate metrics like total stars and active contributors. For detailed reports, she needs access to individual developer profiles and cross-project collaboration patterns. Rachel is highly technical and knows how to identify key technical talent and assess code quality standards.
